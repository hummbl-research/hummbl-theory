# BKI White Paper: Belonging as Knowledge Infrastructure

**Status**: Pre-draft — theoretical corpus assembled, research program defined  
**Target audience**: Enterprise AI governance buyers, Anthropic partner evaluators, academic reviewers  
**Estimated publication**: Q3 2026

## Core Problem Statement

Two independent theoretical traditions converge on BKI's central problem:

1. **Barabási & Albert (1999)** — scale-free networks exhibit preferential attachment: without architectural intervention, epistemic influence concentrates in a small number of dominant models at civilizational scale
2. **Kant (1781)** — cognitive structures constitute rather than merely receive experience: a dominant AI system imposes epistemic categories, not just content

Together: BKI is an **engineering problem with a formal specification** — design network topologies and epistemic infrastructure that resist power-law concentration of constitutive epistemic frames.

## Philosophical Grounding

Ubuntu (Ramose 1999; Tutu 1999): *umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu* — a person is a person through other persons. Knowledge is constituted socially. This is a specific epistemological claim that grounds BKI's inclusivity mandate as structural necessity rather than moral preference.
