## 1. "Mental Model"

**Origin**: Craik, K.J.W. (1943). *The Nature of Explanation*. Cambridge University Press.
**Claim**: The nervous system constructs "small-scale models of reality" to simulate and anticipate events. The mind operates as a physical symbol system.
**Operator**: CO — composite representation (model) composed from sensory inputs and prior knowledge
**Relevance**: Base120 is a typed mental-model library. Craik's 1943 formulation is the primary source for the "mental model" concept used in HUMMBL's theoretical positioning. Johnson-Laird's *Mental Models* (1983) mainstreamed the term in cognitive psychology.

## 2. "World Model"

**Origin**: Polyphyletic — no single coinage.
- Leibniz (~1686): possible worlds (philosophical precursor)
- Wittgenstein, L. (1921). *Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus*. "The world is everything that is the case."
- Tolman, E.C. (1948). "Cognitive Maps in Rats and Men." *Psychological Review*, 55(4), 189–208. (functional precursor: "cognitive map")
- Fikes, R.E. & Nilsson, N.J. (1971). "STRIPS: A New Approach to the Application of Theorem Proving to Problem Solving." *Artificial Intelligence*, 2(3–4), 189–208. (AI crystallization: maintained world-state representation)
- Sutton, R.S. (1990). "Integrated Architectures for Learning, Planning, and Reacting Based on Approximating Dynamic Programming." *ICML Proceedings*. (model-based RL formalization)
- LeCun, Y. (2022). "A Path Towards Autonomous Machine Intelligence." *OpenReview preprint*. (JEPA / modern world model thesis)

**Claim**: Intelligent behavior requires an internal representation of external state whose fidelity to ground truth is continuously maintained.
**Operator**: SY — the world model is a meta-system: a system that represents another system
**Relevance**: HUMMBL's distinction between mental models (reasoning operators) and world models (state representations) is load-bearing for product positioning. HUMMBL is "a governed mental-model layer for reasoning over partial world models" — not a world model itself.

## 3. "Transformation" (domain-specific origins)

### Geometry
**Origin**: Klein, F. (1872). *Vergleichende Betrachtungen über neuere geometrische Forschungen* (Erlangen Programme). Erlangen.
**Claim**: Geometry is the study of properties invariant under transformation groups. Mathematics is characterized by its transformation group, not its objects.
**Operator**: SY — meta-level redefinition of an entire domain
**Relevance**: Base120's 6-operator architecture is a Kleinian geometry of conceptual space: the operators are the transformation group; the 780 models are invariant residues under those transformations.

### Precursors
- Galois, É. (~1832, published 1846). Group theory — non-commutativity as structural richness
- Cayley, A. (1858). "A Memoir on the Theory of Matrices." *Philosophical Transactions*, 148, 17–37.

### Linguistics
**Origin**: Harris, Z. (1957). "Co-occurrence and Transformation in Linguistic Structure." *Language*, 33(3), 283–340.
Chomsky, N. (1957). *Syntactic Structures*. Mouton.
**Claim**: Grammatical sentences are generated by applying transformations to deep structures.

### Physics
- Lorentz, H.A. (1904). Lorentz transformations
- Einstein, A. (1905). Special relativity — invariance under transformation as epistemic foundation
